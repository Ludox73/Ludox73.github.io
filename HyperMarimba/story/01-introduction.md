This webpage is a companion to the paper _Can you hear the shape of a hyperbolic surface? Now for real_. It aims at presenting some of the phenomena we study and some ideas of proofs in a visually engaging way.

The basic idea is to consider a pair $(X, \Gamma)$ where $X$ is a hyperbolic surface and $\Gamma$ is a simple multicurve. Given this data, we associate to any random unit vector a sequence consisting of pairs $(t_i, \gamma_i)_i$ of times and components of $\Gamma$ encountered by the geodesic corresponding to $v$. When we label the components of $\Gamma$ with musical notes, we can think of this sequence as a _melody_ and of $(X, \Gamma)$ as a musical instrument - a _hyperbolic marimba_. The goal of the paper is to investigate to which extent such randomly generated melodies determine the pair $(X, \Gamma)$.
