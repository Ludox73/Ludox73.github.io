Here you can generate _melodies_ starting with two different _marimbas_ $(X, \Gamma)$ and $(X',\Gamma')$. Every time the button to generate the _melody_ is pressed, a new random vector is selected (this is why the corresponding _score_ changes). You can read the _scores_ and listen to the _melody_. You can also numerically estimate the _frequencies_ of _motifs_.

In the default configuration, the density of grey notes is much higher in Marimba A than in Marimba B. This results in melodies which more higher pitch notes. This is independent of the randomly chosen vector. This tells us that _randomly chosen melodies distinguish these two marimbas_.
