The stripes correspond to homotopy classes of arcs. Any two stripes are separated by vectors which never hit another curve.

Something you can see is that hits with angle near $0$ or $\pi$ are less common than others. Ultimately, this is controlled by <i>Santaló's formula</i>, which asserts that the likelyhood of an angle $\theta$ is given by $\vert\sin(\theta)\vert$. You can see below the distribution we obtained numerically: the blue curve is the cumulative distribution function and its derivative&ndash;the density of the distribution&mdash;evidently resembles $\vert\sin\vert$.
