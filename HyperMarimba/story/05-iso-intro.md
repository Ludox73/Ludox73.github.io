Maybe the more surprising example we present in the paper is that there are two non-isometric isomelodic marimbas $(X,\Gamma)$ and $(X',\Gamma')$ where $\Gamma$ is a _separating curve_ and $\Gamma'$ is _non-separating curve_. From _Theorem 1.1_ above we know that for every vector $v$ in a full-measure subset of $T^1X$ there is $v'$ in $T^1X'$ such that the $(X,\Gamma)$ and $(X',\Gamma')$ melodies of $v$ and $v'$ are identical. When you press the _Run Both_ button, such a pair is chosen. In the first diagram you can follow both geodesics for a short time. In the second you can compare the scores.
