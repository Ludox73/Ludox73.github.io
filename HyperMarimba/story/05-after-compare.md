We show that if two marimbas $(X,\Gamma)$ and $(X',\Gamma')$ have the same frequencies for every motif&mdash;we say that they are _isomelodic_&mdash;then for generic vectors $v\in T^1X$ there are vectors $v'\in T^1X'$ such that the corresponding melodies are identical: exactly the same notes played at the same times.

_<b>Theorem 1.1.</b> 
    Two hyperbolic marimbas $(X,\Gamma)$ and $(X',\Gamma')$ are isomelodic if and only if there is a full measure set $U\subset T^1X$ and an absolutely continuous map
    $$\Phi:U\to T^1X'$$
    such that for all $v\in U$ the $\Gamma$-melody of the geodesic in $X$ corresponding to $v$ is exactly the same one that the $\Gamma'$-melody of the geodesic in $X'$ corresponding to $\Phi(v)$: exactly the same notes being played at exactly the same times._
