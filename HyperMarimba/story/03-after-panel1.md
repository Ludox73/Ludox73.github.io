Something one can hear is which notes are being played and how often. More generally one hears the _frequency_ with which a _motif_ is played. A _motif_ is a sequence of notes with predertermined time gaps, with some _tolerance_. Its _frequency_ is the limit
$$\lim_{T\to\infty}\frac {\text{number of times motif played by time }T}T$$
We prove in Proposition 3.3 that the frequencies of motifs do exist&mdash;they might be zero. For example, if you set the _tolerance_ to $0$, then the _frequency_ is $0$: given that even the most accomplished performers diverge slightly from their scores, what can you expect from randomly chosen ones?
